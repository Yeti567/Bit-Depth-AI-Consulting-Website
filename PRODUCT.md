# PRODUCT.md — Bit Depth AI Consulting

## Register
**Brand.** This site IS the product. The marketing surface and the audit deliverable are the same artifact in different forms.

## What we sell
Vendor-neutral AI consulting for Canadian small and mid-sized businesses. We audit before we build. The Audit ($5,000 starting) is the front door; if a client moves to implementation, the audit fee is credited 1:1 against the project.

## Users
Mid-sized Canadian operators across two cohorts that share more than they look like they do:

- **Office operators** — lawyers, accountants, professional-services partners. Educated, document-literate, suspicious of hype, default to the firm-conservative aesthetic.
- **Field operators** — HVAC, plumbing, oilfield service, trades. Owner-operator or ops manager. Time-poor, plain-spoken, allergic to consultant-speak, judge a vendor by whether they return calls.

Both groups distrust software vendors. Both have been pitched AI by someone who didn't understand their business. Both respond to plainness and proof.

## Brand personality
**Contrarian. Warm. Plain-spoken.**

- Contrarian: we will tell you not to buy AI if you shouldn't.
- Warm: a person you can call, not a portal you log into.
- Plain-spoken: no AI-marketing vocabulary. No "transformation," "synergy," "leverage," "unlock," "empower."

## Anti-references
The user did not name competitors but explicitly wants to be unique in the Canadian market. Treat these as the visual / tonal defaults to reject:

- The generic AI-startup template — navy + cyan + dot-grid + lucide icons + three-step process + six-card grid. (This is what the v1 site was.)
- Big-four / IBM consulting — institutional stock photos, generic "transformation" copy, navy suit aesthetic.
- AI hype landing pages — purple-blue gradients, glassmorphism, "10× ROI" hero metrics, gradient text on numbers.
- Agency portfolios — over-designed scroll choreography, oversized typography as decoration, "we craft experiences."

## Strategic principles
1. **Audit-first, build-second.** Diagnosis before prescription. The audit is the product; implementation is the option that follows.
2. **Honesty is the deliverable.** Telling a client they're not ready for AI is a feature, not a failed sale.
3. **Founder-led, lean.** Blake plus a small bench of trusted consultants. Not an agency. Not a software vendor.
4. **Canadian SMB economics.** Enterprise-quality thinking at $5k-$50k engagements, not $500k.
5. **Plain language is the brand.** If a sentence could appear in any other consultant's deck, rewrite it.

## What success looks like
A prospect lands on the homepage, reads two sections, and either books the audit or correctly self-disqualifies. The site is a filter, not a funnel.

## Buyer journey on the site
1. Homepage establishes the POV (audit-first, vendor-neutral, honest).
2. AI Audit page explains the artifact and the $5k starting price.
3. Discovery call or audit booking.

The Readiness Assessment (`/ai-readiness-assessment`) is a separate, free top-of-funnel quiz with its own dark UI — distinct enough from the marketing surface that they should not blend visually.
